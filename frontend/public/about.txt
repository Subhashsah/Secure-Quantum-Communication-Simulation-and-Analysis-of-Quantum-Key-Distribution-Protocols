Secure Quantum Communication: Simulation and Analysis of Quantum Key Distribution Protocols

Abstract
With the rapid development of quantum computing technologies, conventional cryptographic systems based on computational complexity are increasingly vulnerable to cryptanalytic attacks. Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) offers a fundamentally secure approach to key exchange by leveraging the principles of quantum mechanics. This project presents the simulation, visualization, and comparative analysis of four widely studied QKD protocols—BB84, B92, E91, and BBM92. An interactive graphical user interface (GUI) is developed to enhance conceptual understanding and provide an intuitive representation of protocol operations, key generation, and eavesdropping detection mechanisms.

2. Aim and Objectives
2.1 Aim
The primary aim of this project is to simulate and analyze selected Quantum Key Distribution protocols to demonstrate their security properties and operational behavior under ideal and adversarial conditions.

2.2 Objectives
To implement simulations of BB84, B92, E91, and BBM92 protocols
To analyze key generation efficiency and error rates
To model eavesdropping scenarios and observe quantum disturbances
To develop an interactive UI for protocol visualization and comparison
To enhance understanding of quantum communication principles through simulation

3. Motivation
The advent of quantum computing poses a significant threat to classical public-key cryptosystems. QKD represents a promising solution for post-quantum security by providing provably secure key exchange mechanisms. The motivation for this project lies in bridging the gap between theoretical quantum cryptography and practical understanding through simulation-based visualization. By presenting protocol operations interactively, the project aims to make complex quantum concepts accessible to students, researchers, and evaluators.

4. Methodology
4.1 Protocol Selection
The following QKD protocols were selected to represent diverse quantum communication paradigms:

BB84 and B92 (prepare-and-measure protocols)
E91 and BBM92 (entanglement-based protocols)

4.2 Quantum State Simulation
Quantum bits (qubits) are simulated using polarization or abstract quantum states. Random bit sequences and measurement bases are generated to model realistic quantum transmission behavior.

4.3 Key Generation and Sifting
After transmission, the sender and receiver publicly compare measurement bases via a classical channel. Bits corresponding to matching bases are retained to form the raw secret key.

4.4 Eavesdropping Analysis
An adversarial entity is simulated to intercept quantum states. The resulting measurement-induced disturbances are analyzed using error rates to detect the presence of eavesdropping.

4.5 User Interface and Visualization
A graphical user interface presents step-by-step protocol execution, graphical representations of key generation, and comparative performance analysis of the protocols.

5. Results and Discussion
The simulations successfully demonstrate the operational principles and security characteristics of all four QKD protocols. The presence of an eavesdropper introduces measurable errors in the shared key, validating the theoretical security guarantees of quantum cryptography. Entanglement-based protocols exhibit strong correlation properties, while prepare-and-measure protocols highlight simplicity and practical feasibility. The visualization framework significantly enhances user comprehension of abstract quantum processes.

6. Conclusion
This project validates Quantum Key Distribution as a robust and secure approach to cryptographic key exchange. Through simulation and visualization of BB84, B92, E91, and BBM92 protocols, the study demonstrates the effectiveness of quantum principles in detecting eavesdropping and ensuring communication security. The developed UI serves as an educational and analytical tool, emphasizing the relevance of quantum cryptography in future secure communication systems.

Glossary of Key Terms
Quantum Cryptography: A cryptographic technique that utilizes quantum mechanical principles to secure communication.

Quantum Key Distribution (QKD): A method for secure key exchange using quantum states.

Qubit: The fundamental unit of quantum information capable of superposition.

Superposition: A quantum property allowing a qubit to exist in multiple states simultaneously.

Entanglement: A quantum phenomenon in which particles exhibit correlated behavior regardless of separation distance.

Eavesdropping: Unauthorized interception of communication, detectable in QKD due to quantum measurement disturbances.

Quantum Channel: A communication medium used to transmit quantum states.

Classical Channel: A conventional communication channel used for public discussion after quantum transmission.
